# An Teanga Bheo - Gaeilge Chléire
## 1. Stress
**1.1** Stress is on the second syllable if there's a long vowel in that syllable (the stressed syllable is in bold):
+ *cai**lín**, tái**lliúir**, ga**lún**ach, cai**pí**ní, cea**nnód**sa, bá**dóir**eacht, nó**tál**tha, cea**nnaí**míd.*

**1.1. 1** The stress is on the second syllable if the syllable ends with *- ch(t):*
+ *ba**cach**, bea**nnacht**, cio**tach**, cruit**neacht**, ma**llacht**, nea**dacha**, spei**reach**a, cuil**eacht**a.*

**1.1. 2** But this rule does not apply

(i) If *-th-* is in the middle of the word:
+ ***cea**thach, **sai**theach, **fa**thach;*

(ii) if the vowel in the first syllable is long:
+ ***céirs**seach, **brón**ach;*

(iii) if it's an abstract noun
+ ***deis**eacht, **troim**eacht, **tinn**eacht.*

**1.2** The stress is on the third syllable, if that syllable is long and if the two before it are short:
+ *Aib**reán** (abo-), ama**dán**, bal**bhán** (balov-), sim**néi**theacha (simi-).*

**1.3** The stress is on the first syllable in all other cases usually:
+ ***ca**pall, **to**bar, **oí**che, **Mái**re, **deoch**anna, **faill**theacha, **carr**aigreacha, **méa**racán, **iasc**airí.*

**1.3.1** The stress is on the first syllable in these borrowed words:
+ ***gi**ní, **fais**eantaí, **tor**napsaí, **bearr**aicí, **min**istrí.*

**1.4** The rules for stress in verbs aren't the exact same:
+ (i) in verbs of the first conjugation, the stress is always on the first syllable:
	+ ***cuir**tear, **cuir**imíd, **chuir**fá, **buail**tear, **buail**fimíd;*
+ (ii) but when there's an emphatic suffix attached to the long syllable, the long syllable is stressed:
	+ *cuiri**míd**ne, chuir**fá**sa, buailfi**míd**ne, bhuail**fá**sa;*
+ (iii) in verbs of the second conjugation, the stress is always on the long syllable of the stem:
	+ *ceann**aíonn** siad, cheann**aí**dís, ceann**ó**far, cheann**ói**dís.*

**1. 5** The stress patterns in single words and phrases or sentences aren't always the same. See the following contrasts:
+ *sea**gaí** ~ saol na **sea**gaí; po**taí** ~ ag tarrac **pho**taí*
+ *gea**taí** ~ na **gea**taí óir; meas**cán** ~ **meas**cán mearaí*
+ *prá**taí**, cár**taí** ~ mara mbe sé chuin **prá**taí, be sé chuin **cár**taí.*

## 2. Pronunciation
**2. 0** The main pronunciation rules that pertain to the dialect are shown as changes that are applied to the base written form (a > b).

**2. 1 Dh, Gh**

**2. 1. 1** (broad) -dh > ∅
+ *samhradh* > swro
+ *geimhreadh* > gíri
+ *cogadh* > cogo
+ *codladh* > colo

**But**

(i) Nouns
+ *bogradh* > bogorog
+ *glasradh* > glasorog
+ *scanradh* > scwrov
+ *lonradh* > lúrov

(ii) Verb (autonomous past tense)
+ *do briseadh é* > briseov/briseog
+ *do tógadh é* > tógov/tógog (see 8.2.3)

(iii) verbs (3 p. s. *-adh* )
+ *thigeach* (thigeach), *chuirheach* (chuirfeadh), etc. (see 2.13).

**2.1. 2 (slender) -dh/gh > g´**

(i) noun
+ *lá geimhridh* > *gírig*
+ *aimsir shamhraidh* > hwraig
+ *cruaidh* > cruaig
+ *i ndiaidh* > *i ndiaig*
+ Ó Dálaigh > Ó Dálaig

(ii) verb
+ *cheannaigh* > cheannaig an fear eile é
+ *imeoidh* > imeoig na daoiní

**but**
+ *contae Luimnigh* > contae Luimini
+ *contae Shligigh* > contae Shligi
+ *cheannaigh sé* > *cheanna sé* (see 8.2.3)
+ *imeoidh sí* > *meo sí* (see 8.2.4)

**2. 1. 3 -dh-/-gh-**

-dh-/-gh- is dropped and a long vowel or diphthong is said in its place:
+ a, ai > y: *gadhar* (gyr), *maighdean* (mydean);
+ ea, ei > y: *leadhb* (leyb), *leigheas* (leys);
+ o > w: *bodhar* (bwr), *rogha* (rw), *togha* (tw);
+ *ai* > í: *snaidhm* (snaím), *maide draighin* (druín), *saighead* (suíod).
See also: *carghas* > caraghas > caraíos

**2. 2 -bh-/-mh-**

**2. 2. 1** -bh-/-mh- is dropped, especially after long vowels:
+ *ábhacht* > ácht
+ *craobhacha* > craocha
+ *fómhar* > fór
+ *gníomhartha* > gníortha
+ *lámha* > lá
+ *na láimhe* > lá
+ *préamhacha* > pnéacha
+ maidí rámha > rá
+ na róimhe > ró
+ sléibhte > sléite

**2. 2. 2** Between short vowels, it becomes a long vowel or diphthong:
+ a/o > w: *gabhar* (gwr), *amhlaidh* (wluig), *domhan* (dwn);
+ ei/ai > y: *deimhin* (deyn), *taibhreamh* (tyreov);
+ ei > í: *geimhreadh* (gíri), *nimhe* (ní);
+ u > ú: *dubha* (dú), *subhach* (súch);
+ o > ó: comhairle (cóirle), comharsa (córsa).

**2. 2. 3** -abha-/-amha- > ú
+ *an gcualabhair?* > a gcualúir (see 8. 2. 3.)
+ *dealbhas* > dealabhas > dealús
+ *garbha* > garabha > garú
+ *cearrbhach* > cearrabhach > cearúch
+ *searbha* > searabha > searú
+ folmha > folamha > folú
+ greanmhar > greannamhar > greannúr

**2. 2. 4** -ibhe-/-imhe- > í
+ *na Gaillimhe* > Gaillí
+ *duibhré* > duibhiré > duíré
+ *tráigh gainimhe* > gainí
+ *nimhneach* > nimhineach > níneach

**2. 3 Devoicing**

**2. 3. 1** -bth-, -dth-, -gth- > p, t, c
+ *lúbtha* > lúpa
+ *séidthe* > séite
+ *tógtha* > tóca

See also: *rath Dia* (tia) *ar an obair, oíche mhaith díbh* (tíbh) and 8. 2. 4 below.

**2. 4 Short stressed vowels before -ll, -m -nn, -rr**

**2. 4. 1** Said as a long vowel or diphthong:
+ a > á: *barr* (bár), *gearr* (geár);
+ i > í: *im* (ím), *binn* (bin), *coill* (coíl);
+ a/o > w: *am* (wm), *poll* (pwl), *trom* (trwm);
+ ai/ei > y: *faill* (fyl), *seinn* (seyn);
+ ea > w: *ceann* (cewn), *feall* (fewl);
+ io/o > ú: *anonn* (anún), *com* (cúm), *fionn* (fiún).

**2. 4. 2** This rule doesn't apply if the consonant is followed by a vowel; That means that there are regular contrasts in the grammar between diphthongs/long vowels and short vowes. See, for example:
+ á ~ a: *gearr* (geár) ~ *línte gearra, gearrfar* (geár -) *~ gearrann sé;*
+ í ~ i: *im* (ím) *~ punt ime,* binn (bín) *~ níos binne, coill (coíl) ~ gearradh na coille, fillfimíd* (fíl -) *~ ag filleadh;*
+ w ~ o: *trom* (trwm) ~ *daoiní troma, crom* (crwm) *síos ~ ag cromadh;*
+ w ~ a: *do gheall sé* (ghewl) *~ geallann sé, cam* (cwm) *~ cama;*
+ y ~ a: *faill* (fyl) *~ bruach na faille;*
+  y ~ e: *seinn* (seyn) *~ seinneann sé.*

**2. 4. 3** This rule also applies in syllables (i) where another consonant follows those consonants (*ll, m, nn, rr*) and (ii) in the clusters *-ls-, -lt-, -mp-, -ms-, -nc-, -nd-, -ng, -nl-, -nr-, -ns-, -nt-, -rd, -rl-, -rn-, -rs-.* According to that, see: 
+ a > w: *camtha* (cwmho), *canda* (cwndo), *gandal* (gwndol), *langa* (lwnggo), *anlan* (wlon), *anró* (wró), *banrí* (bwrí), *scanradh* (scwrov), *antúr* (wntúr), *altán* (wlhán);
+ a(i) > á: *árd, árdóg, in áirde, thárla, táirseach;*
+ ai > y: *faillteacha* (fylheacha), *aimsir* (ymsir), *laincis* (lyngcis), *baintreach* (byntreach);
+ ea > w: *dealramh* (dewrov), *seang* (sewngg);
+ ea > á: *deárna, beárna;*
+ ei > é: *ball scéirdiúil, céird bheatha*
+ i > í: *milseáin* (mísleáin), *fillte* (fílhi), *tímpeall*, *rínce* (ríngce), *ínsint*, *pínse*, *ínti*, *pínt.*
+ io > ú: *piont* (piúnt), *iontu* (únto), *iompar* (úmpor);
+ o > ú: *ponc* (púngc), *long* (*lúngg*), *contuird* (cúntúird), *cupord* (copúrd), *compord* (cúmpórd);
+ o(i) > ó: *órd, sórd, tóirneach, dóirse;*
+ oi > í: *coínle, soilse* (soísle), *sa loing* (luíngg);
+ oi > u: *oird* (úird), *ceann soird* (súird), *ceann an bhoird* (bhúird);
+ u > ú: *púmpa, úmpa, uncail* (úngcail), *úrlár.*

**2. 5 -lt-, -lt > lh**

(i) *altán* (wlhán), *coltar* (cwlhor), *fáilte* (fáilhe), *faillteacha* (fyheacha), *fáltas* (fálhas), *mílte* (mílhi), *nótálta* (nótáltha);

(ii) *sult* (sulh), *alt* (alh), é a cheilt (cheilh), *coirce a mheilt* (veilh).

**2. 5. 1** But this rule doesn't apply in verbal nouns ending in *-lt* when the *-t* is an ahistoric innovation:
+ *é a fháilt, é a thógáilt, an doras a oscailt* (a scoilt).

**2. 6** -s > -s´ before *c´-, d´-, l´-, n´-, t´-*
+ *tinneas cinn* (tinis), *an cleas céanna* (an cleais), *os cionn* (ais), *flaitheas Dé* (flaihis), *táim in amhras de* (wrais), *taobh theas d' Éirinn* (hais), *níl aon bhunús leis* (bhunúis), *suas leat* (suais), *tá agus níl* (agais), *fuacht agus teas* (agais).

**2. 7 Delenition**

**2. 7. 1** The consonants bh-, mh-, ph- are delenited after words which include the personal pronoun *mo:*
+ *im bhéal* (im béal), *im bheathaidh* (im b-), *im bhaile féin* (im b-), *dom bhodhradh* (om b-), *dom mholadh* (om b-), *im mheoin* (im b-), *im mhacha* (om b-), *im mhéaraibh (im b-)*, *im phóca* (om p-), *dom phógadh* (om p-).

See also: *liom féin* (péin), *dom féin* (péin), etc.

**2. 7. 2** th- is delenited (in the adverbs *thiar, thoir, thuaidh, theas*) after the consonants -n/-s:
+ *an ceann tiar, an ceann toir, an tigh sin toir, an ball san tuaidh, an doras tuaidh, theas is tuaidh, an ceann tuas, an múinteoir sin tuas, í sin teas.*

**2. 8 Metathesis**

**2. 8. 1** C1 + C2 > C2 + C1

(i) -l + s- > -s + l-: *milse* (mísle), *milseáin* (mísleáin), *gailseach* (gysleach);  
(ii) -th + l, n, r- > -l, n, r + th-: *féithleoga* (féiheoga), *cáithleach* (cáilheach), *tráthnóna* (tránhóna), *bóithre* (bóirhi), *aithrí* (airhí), *liathróid* (liarhóid);  
(iii) see also: *cófra* (córha), *fiafraí* (fiarhaí), *deichniúr* (deinhiúr).

**2. 8. 2** C+R+ V - > C+V+R-

(i) In words where the stress is on the second syllable:
+ *breallóg* (bior **lóg**), *bradán* (bor **dán**), *bratach* (bor **tach**), *bradach* (bor **dach**), *brollach* (bor **lach**), *braillín* (bar **lín**), *bruitíneach* (buir **tín** each), *cruitneacht* (kor **neacht**), *crotúil* (cor **túil**), *praiseach* (poir **seach**), *progóid* (por **góid**), *urchóideach* (ro **chóid** each);

(ii) see also: *achrannach* (achornach), *anraithe* (anairthe), *banaltra* (banortla), *colmóir* (clomóir), *druideann* (dirdeann), *dreanncaide* (dearainte), *stoirm* (stroim), *urchar* (rochar).

**2. 9 Epenthetic Vowel (C C > C V C)**

**2. 9. 1** l, n, r + b, g, bh, ch, dh, gh, mh
+ *Alba* (Alobo), *dealg* (dealog), *dealbh* (dealov), *ag inbhear* (eg ineabhor), *inchinn* (inihin), *borb* (borob), *dearg* (dearog), *garbh* (garov), *doirche* (doirihi).

**2. 9. 2** b, c, d, g + l, n, r
+ *Aibreán* (aborán), *oibre* (ebiri), *bacla* (bakola), *ruicne* (ruikini), *acra* (acoro), *cíocrach* (cíocoroch), *madra* (madoro), *eagla* (agolo), *aigne* (aigini), *nár agra* (agoro).

**2. 9. 3** l, n, m, r + l, n m, r
+ *calm* (kalom), *heilm* (heilim), *ainm* (ainim), *iomlán* (umolán), *simné* (siminé), *lomnocht* (lomonocht), *Luimneach* (Luimineach), *comráda* (comoráda), *iomradh* (umoro), *seomra* (seomoro), *dorn* (doron), *feirim* (feirim), *tormas* (toromos).

**2. 9. 4** th + n, r
+ *aithne* (ahini), *leathnú* (leahonú), *raithneach* (raihineach), *faithne* (fahini), *aithris* (ahiris), *ceathrar* (ceahoror).

**2. 9. 5** See also:
+ *sochraid* (sochoraid), *gasra* (gasora), *glasradh* (glasorog), *admhaíonn* (adovaíonn), *aifreann* (aifireann), *saibhreas* (sevireas).

**2. 10 Syncopation**

**2. 10. 1** The short vowel is dropped in the first syllables of words where the second syllable which start with l, n, r and is stressed:
+ *arán* (rán), *ballach* (blach), *bileoga* (bleoga), *bullóg* (blóg), *coláiste* (cláiste), *collach* (clach), *coráiste* (cráiste), *dorae* (drae), *folláin* (fláin), *maireachtain* (mreachtain), *mallacht* (mlacht), *mullach* (mlach), *oráiste* (ráiste), *paróiste* (próiste), *salach* (slach).

**2. 10. 2** See also: *do chonac* (chnoc), *furaiste* (fruiste), *imigh* (migh), *imithe* (mithe), *inis dom* (nis), *ana-mhilis* (anovlis), *minic* (mic), *oscail* (scuil), *olagóireacht* (logóireacht), *spioraid* (sprid), *stoirm* (struim), *tirim* (trim).

**2. 11** Eclipsis of s-
You can eclipse *s-* in the places where other consonants are lenited in Irish generally. When it's a broad *s-*, you get a sound similar to *z* in the English word *zoo*:
+ *ar an saol* (zaol), *radharc na súl* (zúl), *mara suír* (zuír), *i Sasana* (o zasono);

When it's a slender *s-*, you get a sound similar to the *j* in the French word *je*:
+ *ag an séipéal* (zéipéal), *ár síneadh féinig* (zíne), *dá seinnfeá* (á zeynfá).

**2.12 The Sound *y***
(i) in the first syllable of the verbs *éirigh* (yrigh, yríonn, etc.), *éist* (ystíonn sé, yst leis, eg ysteacht, etc.);

(ii) in the words *aosáid* (ysáid though *éasáid* is heard too), *caorán* (kyrán), *craorac* (kryroc);

(iii) in the words *éinne* (aon duine), though *éinne* is heard too: *geach yni/ geach éinne;*

(iv) the words *féileacán* (peyleacán) and *péire* (fyri);

(v) in the words *tinncéir* (tyn-).

**2.13 Sé/Sí**
These forms have a broad *s-*, when they follow forms of verbs that end in *-dh* (= ch).

(i) 3 p. s. Habitual PastL *do chuireach suí* (do chuireadh sí), *thigeach saé* (thigeadh sé);

(ii) 3 p. s. Subjunctive Past: *á gcuireadh suí* (dá gcuireadh sí), *á dtigeach saé* (dá dtigeagh sé);

(iii) 3 p. s. Conditional: *chuirheach suí* (chuirfeadh sí), *do thiocach sae* (do thiocfadh sé);

(iv) 3 p. u. Imperative: *cuireadh suí isteach é* (cuireadh sí), *tigeach sae abhaile* (tigheadh sé). See 6.1.4
